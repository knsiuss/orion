# Email Draft

## When to Use

Use for:
- Drafting a professional email from a brief
- Rewriting or improving a draft you've written
- Adjusting tone (formal, friendly, assertive, apologetic)
- Generating follow-up emails based on past context

Do NOT use for:
- Sending emails directly (use the email channel with explicit confirmation)
- Mass marketing emails

## Tone Presets

| Tone | Use Case |
|------|---------|
| Professional | Business correspondence, client emails |
| Assertive | Escalations, firm requests |
| Friendly | Colleagues, collaborators |
| Apologetic | Mistake recovery, delay notifications |
| Concise | Busy recipients, C-suite |

## Structure

EDITH follows this email structure:
1. **Subject line** — Clear and specific (not clickbait)
2. **Opening** — Brief context or reference to prior communication
3. **Body** — Main message, one idea per paragraph
4. **Call to Action** — Single, clear ask
5. **Closing** — Appropriate sign-off

## Example Invocations

- "Draft an email to Sarah about delaying the deployment to next Friday."
- "Write a follow-up email for the meeting we had yesterday."
- "Make this email more concise and assertive."
- "Draft an apology email to the client about the downtime."

## What It Does

1. Extracts recipient, purpose, and key points from the brief
2. Pulls relevant context from memory (who is this person, prior interactions)
3. Drafts the email in the specified tone
4. Presents for review before sending
5. Saves the draft to memory for follow-up tracking
